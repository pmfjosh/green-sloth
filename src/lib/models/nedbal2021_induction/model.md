The [Nedbal 2021 (Induction)](https://doi.org/10.1093/plphys/kiab317) model is a phenomenological description of a slower, biphasic induction signal measured in _Chlorella sorokiniana_: a single variable, `Signal`, whose rate of change is a fast-rising exponential term (amplitude `rise`, time constant `tau_rise` ≈ 0.012) minus a much slower-decaying exponential term (amplitude `decline`, time constant `tau_decline` ≈ 120). Integrated over time this produces a signal that shoots up within tens of milliseconds and then relaxes back down over roughly two minutes — the kind of kinetics associated with the 820 nm-transmittance (I820) signal reflecting photosystem I / plastocyanin redox state, as opposed to the monotonically rising, sub-second O-J-I-P fluorescence transient.

As with [Nedbal 2021 (OJIP)](nedbal2021_ojip), this curve is not generated from an explicit exponential-sum formula in Nedbal and Lazár (2021); the paper attributes both the O-J-I-P rise and the I820 signal to "the numerically solved model (Lazár, 2009)" — the 42-ODE mechanistic model of PSII/PSI/plastocyanin electron transport from [Lazár's 2009 Photosynthetica paper](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11099-009-0074-8). This model does not reimplement that network; it is a compact, two-exponential empirical fit standing in for the slower signal it produces. Structurally it mirrors the OJIP model's approach of summing exponential terms in a single reaction rate, but the two are not the same model: OJIP sums three same-signed rising terms to produce a monotonic transient, while this one subtracts a slow decline from a fast rise to produce rise-and-relax kinetics on a very different timescale.

The [Nedbal 2021 (Harmonic)](https://doi.org/10.1093/plphys/kiab317) model implements the frequency-domain approach that is the central contribution of Nedbal and Lazár (2021): instead of exposing _Chlorella sorokiniana_ to a sudden dark-to-light transition, the `Light` input is driven sinusoidally at a chosen `period`, and the resulting fluorescence, `Fit`, is decomposed into a fundamental oscillation plus three upper harmonics (variables `H1`–`H4`), each with its own amplitude (`a1`–`a4`), phase (`p1`–`p4`) and harmonic order (`n1`–`n4`). This mirrors the paper's Equation 1, `Fit = A0 + Σ Ak·sin(2πk·t/T − τk)`, and reflects the paper's method of scanning light modulation frequencies from 1000 to 0.001 Hz on light-acclimated algae, with the appearance of upper-harmonic content used as a signature of nonlinear photosynthetic regulation and feedback.

Unlike its sibling models, this one is **not** derived from Lazár's 2009 mechanistic O-J-I-P/I820 model. It is the paper's own methodological innovation, developed independently to avoid the need for dark acclimation required by time-domain measurements such as those captured in the [Nedbal 2021 (OJIP)](nedbal2021_ojip) and [Nedbal 2021 (Induction)](nedbal2021_induction) models.

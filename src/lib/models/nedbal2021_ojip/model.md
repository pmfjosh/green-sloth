The [Nedbal 2021 (OJIP)](https://doi.org/10.1093/plphys/kiab317) model is a compact, phenomenological description of the O-J-I-P transient — the polyphasic rise of chlorophyll a fluorescence that _Chlorella sorokiniana_ shows during the first ~100 ms to 1 s after a dark-to-strong-light transition. It has a single variable, ChlF, whose rate of change is the sum of three exponentially decaying terms (amplitudes `j`, `i`, `p`, each divided by its own time constant `tau_j`, `tau_i`, `tau_p`). Integrated over time this produces a saturating, triple-exponential rise that traces out the J, I and P inflections of the classic OJIP curve without resolving any of the underlying photosystem chemistry.

Within Nedbal and Lazár (2021), the O-J-I-P rise is not generated from this exponential-sum formula — it is explained by "the numerically solved model (Lazár, 2009)", i.e. the 42-ODE mechanistic model from [Lazár's earlier Photosynthetica paper](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11099-009-0074-8) ("Modelling of light-induced chlorophyll a fluorescence rise (O-J-I-P transient) and changes in 820 nm-transmittance signal of photosynthesis"), which describes electron transport through and beyond photosystem II shaping both the OJIP fluorescence rise and the I820 transmittance signal. This GreenSloth model does not reimplement that mechanistic network; it is instead an empirical curve fit that reproduces the shape of the same transient the 2009 model was built to explain, kept alongside the frequency-domain [Nedbal 2021 (Harmonic)](nedbal2021_harmonic) and time-domain [Nedbal 2021 (Induction)](nedbal2021_induction) models as the dark-adapted, fast-kinetics reference case from the same publication.

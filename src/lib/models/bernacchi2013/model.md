The [Bernacchi 2013](https://doi.org/10.1111/pce.12118) model is a review-and-synthesis presentation of the Farquhar, von Caemmerer & Berry (FvCB) biochemical model of C₃ photosynthesis, tracing how the same steady-state framework is used from the chloroplast all the way up to the ecosystem scale. Net CO₂ assimilation is expressed as the minimum of three potential rates: Rubisco-carboxylation-limited ($W_c$), RuBP-regeneration (electron-transport)-limited ($W_j$), and triose-phosphate-utilization-limited ($W_p$), each written as a function of intercellular CO₂ ($C_i$), photosynthetically active photon flux density (PPFD), and the enzyme capacities $V_{cmax}$, $J_{max}$ and TPU. The GreenSloth implementation follows this classical formulation directly: the light-limited electron transport rate $J$ is obtained from a non-rectangular hyperbola of absorbed light and $J_{max}$, and $A_n$ is the minimum of the three limiting rates minus day respiration $R_d$.

Beyond restating the biochemistry, the paper's contribution is showing how the model's parameters ($V_{cmax}$, $J_{max}$, $\Gamma^*$, $K_c$, $K_o$) can be scaled with temperature and how the resulting leaf-level $A$–$C_i$ curves feed into canopy and land-surface models used for ecosystem- and global-scale predictions of carbon uptake. In GreenSloth it serves as a standard, well-parameterized FvCB-type reference model alongside [FvCB 1980](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00386231) and [Johnson 2021](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11120-021-00840-4), against which extensions of the classical Rubisco-limited/RuBP-regeneration-limited switching can be compared.

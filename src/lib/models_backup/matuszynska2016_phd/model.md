The Matuszynska 2016 (PhD thesis) model is the full photosynthesis model developed in Anna Matuszyńska's dissertation, _Mathematical models of light acclimation mechanisms in higher plants and green algae_ (Heinrich-Heine-Universität Düsseldorf, 2016). Unlike the smaller, NPQ-focused [Matuszynska 2016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bbabio.2016.09.003) model that was published separately, this version resolves the complete chloroplast electron transport chain: photosystem II (PSII) and photosystem I (PSI), the plastoquinone (PQ) pool, the cytochrome b6f complex, plastocyanin, FNR, and ATP synthase. On top of this electron-transport backbone it adds the regulatory and acclimation machinery that the thesis set out to study — non-photochemical quenching via PsbS protonation and the xanthophyll cycle (violaxanthin ↔ zeaxanthin), light-harvesting complex (LHC) state transitions, and cyclic electron flow around PSI.

Because it combines a mechanistic electron-transport chain with the short-term light-acclimation mechanisms of higher plants and green algae, this model is best understood as the conceptual parent of the leaner published NPQ model and a direct predecessor of the [Matuszynska 2019](https://doi.org/10.1111/ppl.12962) model, which extends the same framework towards the Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle. As a dissertation it has no assigned DOI, but it is included in GreenSloth because it captures the most complete form of this widely-used family of light-acclimation models, making it a valuable reference for anyone building on or comparing against the Matuszyńska lineage of photosynthesis models.
